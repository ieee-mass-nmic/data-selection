Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) is rarely deployed as a single configuration: a lab or platform maintains a registry of LoRA ranks, module placements, adapters, (IA)$^3$, and other recipes chosen for different tasks and deployment budgets. Gradient- and influence-based data selectors produce strong subsets but bind to the target PEFT---each new configuration rebuilds a target-specific gradient datastore---so selection cost grows linearly with the registry. Reusing one configuration's selected subset removes that cost but sacrifices quality, because a subset optimized for one trainable subspace is mismatched to the others.

We present PCU-Select, which recasts this per-target recomputation as reusable supervision. It models example utility as a quantity conditioned jointly on the task and a structured PEFT code, builds a shared representation of samples, tasks, and PEFT configurations once offline, and trains a conditional scorer with multi-fidelity utility labels. Transferring to a new target PEFT requires only encoding its PEFT code and task sketch and one forward pass over cached sample features---no per-target gradient datastore.

Across Llama-2-7B, four tasks, and five seen PEFT configurations, PCU-Select improves consistently over PEFT-agnostic selectors and near-ties a per-PEFT LESS reimplementation on aggregate downstream quality while still producing distinct per-target subsets. Its contribution is amortization, not a single-configuration win: after 72.0 GPU-hours of offline supervision, each PEFT--task application costs 0.18 GPU-hours. Serving five PEFTs on four tasks therefore costs 75.6 GPU-hours, versus 158.0 for per-PEFT LESS, and breaks even after 2.33 configurations. A single reused LESS subset falls below the PEFT-agnostic frontier. Near-support configurations transfer zero-shot, while far same-family targets and BitFit benefit from calibration; Prefix/P-Tuning remain a zero-shot failure boundary.
