A lab or platform typically maintains a registry of parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) configurations for different tasks and deployment budgets. Gradient- and influence-based data selectors produce target-specific subsets, but they rebuild a gradient datastore for every configuration; reusing one subset avoids this cost but degrades quality because example utility depends on the trainable subspace. We present PCU-Select, which amortizes data selection by conditioning example utility on the task and a structured PEFT code. PCU-Select learns shared sample, task, and configuration representations from multi-fidelity supervision once offline, then ranks cached sample features for a target configuration without rebuilding gradients. Across Llama-2-7B, four tasks, and five seen PEFT configurations, PCU-Select improves average downstream quality by 0.74 points over the PEFT-agnostic RDS+ selector and near-ties per-PEFT LESS (35.18 versus 35.14). After 144.0 GPU-hours of offline supervision, onboarding a new four-task PEFT costs 1.44 GPU-hours, versus 63.2 for LESS, a $44\times$ reduction; serving all five configurations costs 151.2 GPU-hours instead of 316.0. Near-support configurations transfer zero-shot, while calibration repairs far same-family configurations and BitFit; Prefix/P-Tuning remain outside the supported zero-shot regime.
