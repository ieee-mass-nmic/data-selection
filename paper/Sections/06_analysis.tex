\section{Analysis}
\label{sec:analysis}

\subsection{PEFT Conditioning Produces Target-Specific Subsets}

Across the LoRA scan, matched conditioning beats reuse from another configuration by 1.45 points. PCU-Select averages 21.25, versus 20.70 for LESS and 20.27 for RDS+. Its 21 unordered configuration pairs have mean Jaccard 0.427; PEFT-agnostic RDS+ is exactly 1.000. Configuration distance correlates with selection difference at descriptive Spearman $\rho=0.969$ (leave-one-configuration-out range $[0.947,0.972]$); shared configurations preclude an IID interval (Appendix Figure~\ref{fig:config-sensitivity}). \textbf{Takeaway.} Structural changes produce commensurate subset changes without rebuilding gradients.

\subsection{Ablations Identify the Useful Components}

Appendix Table~\ref{tab:ablations} gives the full ablation. Removing the PEFT code causes the largest drop (1.37 points) and lowers held-out NDCG from 0.702 to 0.526. A family one-hot loses 0.68, confirming that rank, placement, capacity, and recipe add signal; removing the task sketch loses 0.82. Low- and high-fidelity-only variants lose 0.85 and 0.36. Adaptive quotas beat global top-$k$ by 1.08 and uniform quotas by 0.51; the latter has slightly higher NDCG (0.709) but lower downstream quality. \textbf{Takeaway.} Structured conditioning, task relativity, multi-fidelity labels, and coverage each contribute.

\subsection{Transfer Protocol for Unseen PEFTs}

E5 fixes a structural taxonomy before evaluation: L0 contains near-support same-family targets, L1 farther same-family shifts, and L2 unseen families (full registry in Appendix Table~\ref{tab:appendix-peft-registry}). The tiers are qualitative and make no density-estimation claim.

L0 trails LESS by 0.34 points zero-shot. With 500 labels, the L1 gap falls from 2.08 to 0.30 and the BitFit gap from 4.08 to 0.23. Prefix/P-Tuning lack native short-horizon labels and trail RDS+ by 6.97 zero-shot. Calibration costs 0.525 GPU-hours per PEFT--task pair (Figure~\ref{fig:ood-calibration}; Appendix Table~\ref{tab:ood-levels}). \textbf{Takeaway.} Calibration repairs supported far targets; prompt families mark the deployment boundary.

\subsection{Limitations}

The evidence covers one backbone family, four tasks, and a finite registry. Short-horizon labels approximate full-training contribution, with a visible TyDiQA deficit. Main-table dispersion varies target training but fixes selector and scorer state. Decontamination removes 0.58\% of the pool, yet weaker matches may remain. Prefix/P-Tuning fail zero-shot and lack native calibration labels. The cost benefit is relative to per-PEFT LESS; PEFT-agnostic selectors remain preferable when a small quality loss is acceptable.
