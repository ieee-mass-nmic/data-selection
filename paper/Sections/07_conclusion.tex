\section{Conclusion}

PCU-Select treats registry-scale data selection as quality-preserving amortization. Its structural PEFT and task conditions produce target-specific subsets from one scorer. Across four tasks and five seen configurations, it near-ties per-PEFT LESS while reducing selection cost from 158.0 to 75.6 GPU-hours ($2.09\times$) and breaking even after 2.33 configurations. Near-support targets transfer zero-shot; calibration repairs far same-family targets and BitFit, while Prefix/P-Tuning remain a failure boundary. These results position PCU-Select as reusable selection infrastructure for a PEFT registry.
