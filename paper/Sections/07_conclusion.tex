\section{Conclusion}

In this paper, we study cross-PEFT data-efficient fine-tuning, where the goal is to select useful training samples for a target PEFT method under a limited data budget. We show that sample utility can vary across PEFT methods, motivating the need for PEFT-aware data selection.

We propose a PEFT-conditioned utility scoring framework that predicts sample usefulness based on sample, task, and PEFT representations. The learned scorer can be reused across target PEFT configurations and enables efficient subset selection without recomputing utility from scratch for every PEFT method.

Experiments across multiple PEFT configurations demonstrate that the proposed method improves over random selection and provides better cross-PEFT transferability. Further analyses highlight the role of PEFT conditioning, the behavior under unseen PEFT settings, and the computational benefits of reusable utility modeling.

Future work includes extending the framework to larger model families, more diverse PEFT configurations, and online data selection settings where the selected subset is updated dynamically during fine-tuning.
