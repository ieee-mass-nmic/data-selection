\section{Conclusion}

Data selection for a PEFT registry is an amortization problem, not a single-target accuracy race. Existing gradient selectors force a choice between recomputing per target---accurate but linear in registry size---and reusing one subset, which is cheap but PEFT-agnostic in effect and which we measure below the PEFT-agnostic frontier. PCU-Select dissolves that choice by conditioning data utility on the target task and a structured PEFT code: one scorer reuses cached sample and task signatures yet still produces a target-specific subset when the PEFT method, rank, placement, or recipe changes. Across four tasks and five seen configurations it near-ties per-PEFT LESS in quality---the design target under our amortization objective---while cutting five-PEFT selection cost by $2.2\times$ and breaking even after about 2.3 targets. The boundary is also clear: far-OOD PEFT families need a few hundred calibration labels before the offline scorer can be trusted, a low-cost repair that keeps them inside the amortization regime. We therefore position PCU-Select as reusable selection infrastructure for a PEFT registry rather than a single-configuration performance winner.
