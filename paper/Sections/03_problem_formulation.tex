\section{Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:problem}

We formalize \emph{cross-PEFT data-efficient fine-tuning}: selecting a compact training subset whose value depends on the target PEFT configuration and target task. Fix a backbone family and frozen base model $M_0$. A candidate pool $\mathcal{D}=\{x_i\}_{i=1}^{N}$ contains instruction-response examples. A task $t$ provides a small validation sketch $V_t$ drawn from train or development data, never from the test set. A PEFT configuration $p\in\mathcal{P}$ specifies the family, trainable locations, capacity, and training recipe. Given budget $B$, the ideal subset solves
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{S}^{*}=\arg\max_{\mathcal{S}\subseteq\mathcal{D},\,|\mathcal{S}|\le B}
  \mathrm{Perf}\!\left(\mathrm{Tune}(M_0,p,\mathcal{S}),V_t^{\mathrm{test}}\right).
  \label{eq:selection-objective}
\end{equation}
The held-out set $V_t^{\mathrm{test}}$ appears only in this definition of the oracle; it is never used for training or for selection, which see only the sketch $V_t$. Directly optimizing Eq.~\eqref{eq:selection-objective} is infeasible because it requires training and evaluating many subsets for every target $(p,t)$ pair.

PCU-Select replaces the subset oracle with a conditional per-example utility,
\begin{equation}
  s_{\phi}(x,p,t)\approx u(x,p,t),
  \label{eq:surrogate}
\end{equation}
where $u$ measures how much $x$ helps task $t$ when the trainable parameters are those of $p$. Concretely, $u(x,p,t)\in[0,1]$ summarizes sketch-loss reductions into a rank-normalized short-horizon utility score, as defined in the Method section. Conditioning on $p$ is the core departure from PEFT-agnostic selection. A sample's gradient can be useful in the attention projections trained by LoRA, weak in the residual bottlenecks trained by adapters, and different again under multiplicative (IA)$^3$ gates. The selected subset should therefore change when the trainable subspace changes.

\paragraph{Amortization objective.}
A registry makes cost, not single-target accuracy, the operative criterion. Let $\mathcal{P}_{\mathrm{target}}=\{p_1,\dots,p_T\}$ be the target PEFT configurations a fixed pool must serve. A per-target influence selector such as LESS pays a selection cost for each configuration, whereas an amortized selector pays a one-time offline cost plus a small online cost per target:
\begin{equation}
  C_{\mathrm{LESS}}(T)=T\, C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{LESS}},
  \qquad
  C_{\mathrm{PCU}}(T)=C_{\mathrm{offline}}+T\, C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{PCU}},
  \label{eq:cost}
\end{equation}
with $C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{PCU}}\ll C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{LESS}}$. The goal is \emph{quality-preserving amortization}: match the per-PEFT selector's downstream quality,
\begin{equation}
  \Delta_{\mathrm{quality}}=\mathrm{Perf}_{\mathrm{PCU}}-\mathrm{Perf}_{\mathrm{LESS}}\approx 0,
  \label{eq:quality-tie}
\end{equation}
while spending strictly less once the registry exceeds the break-even size $T^{\star}=C_{\mathrm{offline}}/(C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{LESS}}-C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{PCU}})$, i.e.\ $C_{\mathrm{PCU}}(T)<C_{\mathrm{LESS}}(T)$ for $T>T^{\star}$. Under this criterion a near-tie with LESS is the design target, not a shortfall: LESS is the quality reference and PCU-Select is its amortized alternative. Reusing a single LESS subset is the degenerate point $C_{\mathrm{offline}}{=}C^{\mathrm{sel}}_{\mathrm{LESS}}$, $C^{\mathrm{sel}}{=}0$ of the same trade-off---cheap, but PEFT-agnostic in effect and therefore quality-losing.

Task relativity is concrete: a worked-solution arithmetic example receives high utility under the GSM8K sketch but low utility under the TyDiQA sketch, because its gradient aligns with the reasoning task but not with multilingual span extraction; the same example's utility also shifts across PEFTs when the aligned sites are not the ones a given method trains.

The formulation rests on four requirements. \textbf{PEFT dependence}: the scorer must take $p$ as an explicit input. \textbf{Task relativity}: utility must be defined relative to $V_t$ so that the model does not learn a generic quality score. \textbf{Structural encodability}: PEFT methods must be represented by their sites, operator type, capacity, and recipe, not just by a family name. \textbf{Amortizable cost}: expensive labels and scorer training are paid offline, while applying to a new $(p,t)$ pair uses cached features and forward scoring.
